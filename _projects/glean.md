### Project Overview
More than half of the data that is generated and stored is never accessed/used again; commonly known as Dark Data. This is true for organisations and individuals, alike. And storage in data centers is energy intensive due to the underlying infrastructure, computation, networking, and cooling costs. Each one of us is generating hundreds of gigabytes of data on a daily basis that ends up in data centers, resulting in the increasing energy consumption of data centers, with estimates indicating that data centers consume about 2% of global electricity. The corresponding carbon emissions are also high reaching up to 3.9% of global carbon emissions (more than aviation). And in Denmark, energy usage of data centers is projected to account for around 15% of the country’s electricity use by 2030. Our aim in this project is to develop methods and practices that will improve the environmental sustainability of data with a focus on frontier AI pipelines.
